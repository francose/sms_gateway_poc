# Empirical gateway recon — 2026-05-23

Raw evidence collected from a residential WSL2 box on a US east-coast
consumer ISP, queried against multiple DNS resolvers. Captured to
verify which carrier email-to-SMS gateways are actually alive in
May 2026, since secondary sources are inconsistent.

## Method

- `dig MX <domain>` via the local stub resolver.
- DNS-over-HTTPS to `1.1.1.1/dns-query` (Cloudflare) as a second
  authoritative check.
- For port-25 reachability, `smtplib.SMTP(host, 25, timeout=8)` from
  WSL2. Note: most US consumer ISPs block outbound 25, so timeouts
  here reflect ISP behavior, not gateway state.

### Zone existence (SOA, to confirm AT&T MX absence is real, not NXDOMAIN)

Both zones are published but contain no MX. AT&T has explicitly

### Cloudmark / Proofpoint ownership of cloudfilter.net

```

     pdns99.ultradns.{biz,com,net,org}
```

Confirms `*.cloudfilter.net` is operated by Proofpoint. Cloudmark was
acquired by Proofpoint in 2017; the brand has been retired but the
domain continues to host carrier-side mail filtering.

## Empirical send test

- **Sender**: a clean consumer Gmail account via `smtp.gmail.com:587`
  (App Password, 2FA-protected, no abuse history)
- **Recipient**: an authorized test handset on Xfinity Mobile (Verizon
  MVNO), `<10-digit>@vzwpix.com`. Also retried with E.164-prefixed
  11-digit local-part. Both attempts.
- **Subject**: `POC test`
- **Body**: ASCII, no URL shorteners, no phishing lures
- **Send time**: 2026-05-23T21:21Z and 21:27Z
- **SMTP result**: 250 OK from Gmail (handed off cleanly)
- **Delivery**: did not arrive on the recipient handset within ~10
  minutes. No bounce received in the sender's Gmail inbox.

Conclusion: Cloudmark/Proofpoint accepts the message via Gmail's
trusted source, then silently drops before SMSC injection. Likely
DMARC alignment evaluation: `From: @gmail.com` does not align with the
sending IP's expected reputation, and Cloudmark policy for carrier
gateways is "consumer mail → drop without notification".

## Implications

1. **AT&T retired the gateway entirely.** No MX, no mail accepted.
   Any third-party documentation still listing `txt.att.net` or
   `mms.att.net` as active is wrong.
2. **Verizon did not kill vtext.com.** Contrary to widely-cited 2022
   reports, the SMS gateway zone still publishes MX records — pointing
   at Proofpoint's Cloudmark. What was actually killed was open
   accept-and-deliver behavior. The bridge persists for authenticated
   business senders.
3. **T-Mobile, Verizon SMS, and Verizon MMS share filtering infra.**
   All three carriers' surviving gateways resolve to
   `*.cloudfilter.net` (Proofpoint). Single point of inspection.
4. **Consumer SMTP sources cannot deliver in 2026.** Gmail-originated
   mail is silently dropped at the carrier MTA for all three
   Proofpoint-fronted gateways. The vector requires a sender domain
   with full SPF/DKIM/DMARC alignment and a clean IP reputation —
   essentially an owned MTA on a non-residential network.
5. **US Cellular is the outlier.** Their gateway runs on their own
   `mx.uscc.net` infrastructure, not Proofpoint. Filtering posture
   not characterized in this recon.

## Reproduction

```bash
for d in txt.att.net mms.att.net vtext.com vzwpix.com tmomail.net \
         msg.fi.google.com email.uscc.net mms.uscc.net \
         sms.cricketwireless.net messaging.sprintpcs.com; do
  printf "%-32s " "$d"
  curl -s -H "accept: application/dns-json" \
       "https://1.1.1.1/dns-query?name=$d&type=MX" \
    | python3 -c "import sys,json; r=json.load(sys.stdin); \
                  a=r.get('Answer',[]); \
                  print('answers:', len(a)); \
                  [print('  ',x['data']) for x in a]"
done
```
